hw-03: Practicing D3 (RW, AP, and WT)

Our first visualization represents a grouped bar chart showing the relationship of different cars their countries of origin, with respect to horsepower. We selected this data set because we am both interested in cars and statistics surrounding them, and additionally, it was simple to navigate and use. https://perso.telecom-paristech.fr/eagan/class/igr204/datasets is the link where we found the original data set, under "Cars". A grouped bar chart is appropriate and optimal for this data, because it takes two categorical data points (Car Maker and Country of Origin), and visualizes one quantitative trend (Horsepower). Thus, the visualization above precisely demonstrates that, in an intuitive and aesthetically-pleasing manner.

Viz 1



Our second visualization is a line chart conveying the trend of graduation rates among high schools in Tempe, Arizona over time, from 2012-2020. We chose this data set on behalf of our interest in education and student performance, especially in a niche city like Tempe. The initial link to this data set is https://catalog.data.gov/dataset/3-08-high-school-graduation-rates-summary. The choice to visualize this data through a line chart is ideal, as it is displaying a key trend over time, which can best be seen through the lens of a line chart. Therefore, the visualization below is extremely effective, and we hope it achieves the goal of highlighting a unique pattern over time.
